// KNOW THE SHAPE OF IT

The scams doing the most damage right now

Attackers don't usually need to break your device — they need you to make one quick decision under pressure. Here's what that pressure looks like.

📱

OTP & call fraud

A caller poses as your bank or telecom, creates urgency, and asks you to read out a one-time code. No real bank ever needs your OTP over the phone.

High impact
📦

Fake courier & delivery links

An SMS claims a parcel is stuck over a small unpaid fee. The link leads to a page built to steal your card details, not release any package.

High impact
💼

Job & earn-from-home scams

You're "selected" for a job with no interview, then asked for a refundable deposit or your CNIC details upfront to "process" your onboarding.

Medium impact
🔁

SIM swap attempts

A call claims your SIM will be blocked unless you share a code or your CNIC. The real goal is hijacking your number to receive your OTPs.

High impact
🎉

Prize & lottery messages

A message says you've won a draw you never entered. Claiming it requires paying a "release fee" or sharing personal and banking details.

Medium impact
👥

Hijacked friend accounts

A compromised friend's account messages you asking to forward a login code "sent by mistake" — it's your account being targeted next.

// IF IT ALREADY HAPPENED

Where to actually report it in Pakistan

If you've been scammed or think your account is compromised, act fast — most banks can only reverse a transaction within a short window.

FIA Cyber Crime Wing (NR3C)

Pakistan's federal agency for cybercrime complaints — phishing, hacking, financial fraud, and online harassment. File a complaint through their official helpline or nearest zonal office.

Your bank's fraud/helpline

Call the number printed on your card or official app — never a number sent to you in a text — and freeze the account immediately if you shared an OTP or card details.
